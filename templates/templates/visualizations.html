Alternative Approaches (for reference)

1. Ternary Plot

The mathematically correct representation. Each corner is 100% of one outcome. The path shows how probabilities evolve over time.

Pros
  • Perfectly preserves constraint
  • No redundancy
  • Shows all three equally
Cons
  • Less familiar to users
  • Time not on x-axis

2. Stacked Area Chart

Shows how probability mass shifts between outcomes over time. Stack always sums to 100%.

Australia
Draw
England
Pros
  • Extremely readable
  • Time on x-axis
  • Shows shifts clearly
Cons
  • Middle series harder to read
  • Order affects perception

3. Two-Axis Reduction

Reduces to 2D: X = Australia − England, Y = Draw. A single point that moves over time.

Pros
  • Compact single point
  • Shows trajectory
  • Mathematically complete
Cons
  • Not intuitive
  • Requires explanation

4. Confidence Line + Draw Band

Main line shows Aus vs Eng (normalized excluding draw). Shaded band shows draw probability.

Aus vs Eng
Draw %
Pros
  • Familiar mental model
  • Easy to scan
  • Matches betting intuition
Cons
  • Draw is second-class
  • Technically lossy
